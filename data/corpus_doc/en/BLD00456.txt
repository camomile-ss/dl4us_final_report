kitayama juhachi kento
kitayama juhachi kento was a welfare facility that was constructed in nara kitamachi in the kamakura period for protecting and aiding persons in serious illness,such as hansen's disease.
on may 3,1921,the site was designated as a national historical site.
location
454 kawakami-cho,nara city,nara prefecture
summary
this facility was built by ninsho in 1243 at saidai-ji temple.
initially,it was built in the northeast of hannya-ji temple.
however,it wad destroyed by fire in a war in 1567,and then was reconstructed in the present site in the kanei era(1661-1672).
it was repaired in 1693.
with a length of approx.37 meters in the east-west direction,the building was partitioned into 18 rooms.
with each room provided with a space of around two tatami mats(except one room with a space of approx.four mats),the inmates of the facility were provided with food,clothing and shelter.
it is said that 18,000 persons in total lived in the facility.
