tokusei ikki(tokusei uprising)
a tokusei uprising is a type of peasant uprising.
the purpose of these uprisings was for a proclamation of tokuseirei(ordering the return of sold land and a dissolution of debts).
tokusei uprisings frequently occurred during the muromachi period beginning with the shocho no doikki(peasant uprising of the shocho era)in 1429 which took a long time to subside.
a famous uprising among "tokusei ikki" was the one that occurred during the kakitsu era.
among the various causes behind a tokusei ikki include poor harvests,infectious diseases,a declining population and a lack of trust in the shogun.
participants in an uprising included bashaku(shipping agents who used horses),toiya(warehouse merchants)and toimaru(specialized wholesale merchants).
