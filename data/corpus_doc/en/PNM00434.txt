zenami
zenami(1386-1482)was a gardener who lived during the muromachi period.
although he was a kawaramono(discriminated position),the 8th shogun yoshimasa ashikaga of the muromachi bakufu(japanese feudal government headed by a shogun)treated him preferentially.
the works attributed to zenami include inryo-ken house-hold in the shokoku-ji temple of 1458,hananogoshosen-den palace of 1461,takakuragosho-sensui fountain of 1462 and yamauchisuiin-ken house-hold in the shokoku-ji temple of 1466.
he moved to nara city during the onin war and worked on daijo-in in the kofuku-ji temple.
the year of birth is estimated 1393 from the fact that he died at the age of 97.
his son,koshiro also served as gardener and the garden in the ginkaku-ji temple is made by his children,jiro and saburo,and his grandson,matashiro.
